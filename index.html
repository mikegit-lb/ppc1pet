Present Perfect Continuous

1. Main Meaning

The Present Perfect Continuous connects the past to now. It usually shows an action that started in the past and is still continuing, or an action that has recently stopped but has a visible result now.

Turkish note: “Bir süredir yapıyorum / yapmaktayım” anlamına gelir. Türkçedeki “-dır/-dir” veya “bir süredir” fikrine yakındır.

Form: Subject + have/has + been + verb-ing

I have been studying English for two years.
She has been watching videos since 8 p.m.
They have been practicing for the PET exam.

2. Positive, Negative, and Question Forms

Positive

I/You/We/They have been studying. He/She/It has been studying.

Negative

I/You/We/They haven’t been studying. He/She/It hasn’t been studying.

Question

Have you been studying? Has she been studying?

Turkish note: “Have/has” burada yardımcı fiildir. Türkçeye genellikle çevrilmez. Ana anlam “been + V-ing” yapısından gelir.

3. For and Since

Use for with a period of time: for two hours, for three days, for a long time.

Use since with a starting point: since Monday, since 2022, since I was ten.

Turkish note: for = ne kadar süredir? since = ne zamandan beri?
I have been learning English for three years.
I have been learning English since 2023.

4. Present Perfect Continuous vs Present Perfect Simple

Use Present Perfect Continuous to focus on the action, duration, effort, or temporary activity.

Use Present Perfect Simple to focus on completion, result, number, or achievement.

Continuous: I have been reading this book all week.
Focus: the activity and time.
Simple: I have read three chapters.
Focus: completed amount.
Continuous: She has been writing emails all morning.
Focus: effort/activity.
Simple: She has written five emails.
Focus: result/number.
Turkish note: “I have been doing” süreç vurgular. “I have done” sonuç vurgular.

5. Common PET B1 Mistakes

❌ I have been know him for years.
✅ I have known him for years.
Some stative verbs are not normally used in continuous forms.
❌ She has been study since morning.
✅ She has been studying since morning.
❌ I am studying English since 2022.
✅ I have been studying English since 2022.
❌ How long do you learn English?
✅ How long have you been learning English?

Reading Comprehension: “The Exam Project”

Focus: the difference between Present Perfect Continuous and Present Perfect Simple.

Leo is fifteen, and he has been preparing for the PET exam for nearly six months. At first, he only studied grammar rules, but recently he has been trying to improve his speaking and writing too. His teacher, Ms. Carter, has noticed that Leo has become more confident, especially when he talks about school, hobbies, and future plans.

For the last three weeks, Leo has been working on a special English project with two classmates, Maya and Deniz. They have been creating a short video series called “Real English for Teens.” In each video, they explain one useful grammar point and give examples from everyday life. So far, they have recorded four videos, written twelve short dialogues, and uploaded two episodes to their class platform.

Maya has been focusing on pronunciation because she often speaks too quickly. She has practiced difficult sounds every evening, and her pronunciation has improved a lot. Deniz has been collecting useful PET vocabulary from films, songs, and online articles. He has already made a vocabulary notebook with more than 120 words and phrases.

Leo, however, has been feeling a little tired this week. He has spent many hours editing the videos, and he has not finished the final episode yet. His eyes are red because he has been looking at the computer screen for too long. Ms. Carter told him, “You have done excellent work, but you also need rest. Remember, continuous forms often show effort and duration. Simple perfect forms often show results.”

On Friday, the students showed their project to the class. Their classmates loved it. Leo realized something important: he had not just learned grammar; he had been using English to create something real. Since then, he has been speaking more bravely in class, and he has decided to keep making English videos after the PET exam.
